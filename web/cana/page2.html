Carbon Neutral Market and Business Opportunities

Market Overview

Countries in Southeast Asia rely on fossil fuels to meet their rising energy needs. Threequarters of the increase in energy demand, which is expected to increase by around 3% annually, will be satisfied by fossil fuels. As a result, compared to 2020, CO2 emissions in Southeast Asian nations will rise by 35%. At the same time, China has surpassed all other countries to take the top spot in terms of carbon emissions, contributing one-third of global CO2 emissions.

The ASEAN region benefits from the development of a green economy and clean energy because it is rich in natural resources. In July 2022, China and ASEAN will accelerate the development of a comprehensive strategic partnership to fulfill the objectives of the United Nations 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and the Paris Agreement in order to promote the bio-circular green economy and low-carbon energy transformation. By 2030, carbon dioxide emissions are expected to be 20% lower than they are today. The whole quantity of carbon dioxide created by humans, both directly and indirectly, is offset by afforestation, energy saving, emission reduction, and various new energy production or storage methods, which is known as "carbon neutrality".

Case Study : Tesla, Inc

The automotive division reported US$16.861 billion in revenue for the first quarter of 2022, an increase of 87% from US$9.002 billion in the same period of 2021, and US$3.318 billion in net profit, an increase of 657.53% year over year, according to Tesla (TESLA). The sales revenue of carbon credits was 679 million US dollars year 2022, an increase of 31.1% compared with 518 million US dollars in the same period of 2021! 20% of the overall profit comes from the sale of carbon credits. It is evident that purchasing and selling carbon credits is a lucrative industry. From 2012 to 2021, Tesla's cumulative carbon credit sales revenue reached US$5.34 billion. As of the first quarter of 2022, carbon credit revenue has reached 6.19 billion US dollars! Among them, Tesla's carbon credit revenue in 2020 was 1.58 billion US dollars, setting a record for the highest carbon credit revenue in the past 10 years. It can be seen that buying and selling carbon credits is good business—a big business!

Carbon Trading Opportunity

The maximum amount of carbon that can be released serves as a quota, and each country has its own carbon emission cap. Some countries with remarkable environmental protection policies have actual carbon emissions below the quotas, enabling them to sell their unused emissions to countries with actual carbon emissions beyond the given quotas. Trading carbon dioxide emission rights as a commodity in state-approved organizations is known as carbon trading. If a company's carbon dioxide emissions go beyond this limit, it must purchase carbon quotas from the market. However, if a company can reduce its carbon emissions below its quota through the use of energy-saving and emission-reduction technologies, it can sell the excess through the carbon trading market for a profit.

The Carbon Peak

The global carbon dioxide concentration will spike between 2020 and 2030 at 11.6 billion tons (carbon dioxide emissions will peak, and China guarantees that before 2030 year, emissions will not increase and will steadily decline after reaching the peak). To become carbon neutral after 2030, 11.6 billion tonnes of carbon dioxide must be neutralised. By the year 2022, 1.2 billion US dollars' worth of carbon emission allowances totaling 194 million tons would have been exchanged on the Chinese carbon market.
In 2022, the average carbon price in China is expected to be $7 per ton, rising to $12.50 per ton by 2025, and to $20 per ton by 2030. $20 multiplied by 11.6 billion tons equals US$232 billion. There will be a shortage of carbon allowances in the future as the peak of 11.6 billion tonnes diminishes. According to this estimation, the market might be worth more than 200 billion US dollars.

Carbon Rights Trading Platform

Singapore created the market for trading carbon rights under the name - CIX. The voluntary carbon trading market was introduced by the Singapore Stock Exchange, DBS Bank, Standard Chartered Bank of the United Kingdom, and Singapore's state-owned investment organization. The primary service clients are large international corporations and institutional investors.

FTIX - In Thailand, the first trading market for carbon credits and renewable energy was launched. The exchange is managed by the Thai Industry Association, which is made up of 12,000 private businesses from 45 different industries. Credits from program to improve energy efficiency, employ renewable energy sources, manage waste, regulate traffic, manage agriculture, manage forests, and more will also be traded on the exchange.
